Opportunities

Graduate Research

Ph.D. Positions — Fall 2026

We are looking for ONE motivated Ph.D. students to join the group in Fall 2026. Successful candidates will conduct original research at the intersection of remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and climate science — with opportunities to contribute to state and federal funded projects on land cover change, climate extremes, and agricultural sustainability.

Students will be fully supported through research assistantships and will have access to IU's high-performance computing resources, a vibrant interdisciplinary research environment, and a strong network of national and international collaborators.

Background

Geography, environmental science, computer science, atmospheric science, or related fields.

Skills

Programming (Python, R, or similar), remote sensing or GIS experience, strong quantitative foundation.

Qualities

Intellectual curiosity, self-motivation, and a genuine interest in geospatial and climate research.

How to apply

Send a brief email outlining your research interests and attach your CV. Formal applications go through IU's graduate admissions.

Undergraduate Research

Undergraduate Research Assistants

We are always looking for motivated undergraduate students interested in working with the group on geospatial and climate research. Undergraduate RAs gain hands-on experience with satellite imagery analysis, GIS, and scientific programming, and often co-author publications and present at conferences.

No prior research experience is required — curiosity and commitment are what matter most.

For all inquiries, please email yaqhe@iu.edu with a brief description of your research interests and attach your CV or resume. We review applications on a rolling basis.
