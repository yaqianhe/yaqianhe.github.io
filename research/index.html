Current Research

Land Cover and Land Use Change

Land cover and land use change is important for global carbon budget, environmental change assessment, agricultural planning, and convervation measures. We use novel machine learning and deep learning algorithms to imporve the accuracy of LCLU data across globe and subsequently detect its changes. The LCLUC patterns, rates, and transitions identified in our studies would be a valuable source of climate and environmental studies. See Publication

Attribution of Climate Varability and Extremes

Climate variability and extremes are a combination of internal variability, GHG emissions, and other forcings, like land cover and land use change (LCLUC). However, how comparable the different forcings is not yet sufficiently understood and quantified. The lack of process understanding and reliable quantification of impacts can be attributed to uncertainties in LCLUC information used in current LCLUC-Atmosphere interactions studies. We take the advantage of LCLUC derived from remotely-sensed data and use robust statistical methods and climate models to attribute the causes of climate variability and extremes. See Publication

Climate Change Impacts

Humanity's combustion of fossil fuels has warmed the planet by ~ 1 ℃. Increases in damaging extremes have already occurred due to this warming and are projected to increase further, irrespective of a 2 ℃ or even a 1.5 ℃ target. Better understanding how climate change impacts on ecosystems, water sources, socioeconomic, etc. could help humans to design feasible mitigation pathways. We utilize future climate data from Coupled Model Intercomparison Projects (CMIP) under different projection scenarios (e.g., RCP4.5) to assess the effects of climate change on a variety of components of natural esystems. See Publication

Remote Sensing of Agricultural Aid

The United Nations has set up Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) to address the necessity of reducing poverty, advancing social equity, and simultaneously ensuring environmental protection. In sub-Saharan Africa, extreme poverty highlights the tension between Agricultural aids and the environment. How these aids impact on the environment is quite ambiguous due to limited long-term data. We use remotely-sensed data and novel causal inference statistical methods to evaluate how agricultural aid impacts on local environment across various countries in the sub-Saharan Africa and and thus achieve UN millennium goals. See Publication

GIS of Human Health

Humanity's health is more vulnerable to environment changes. The pandemic of Covid-19 produces numbers of tragedies in the society.
