Safety in numbers: cost-effective endangered species management for viable populations

In Land Economics (2019). With Lucas Bair, Michael Springborn, and Charles Yackulic.

The economic benefits of species preservation are often difficult to estimate, but we can make use of a viability (abundance) target that implies an estimable value of avoiding population collapse. Here, we use a new chance-constrained dynamic programming algorithm to jointly estimate the least-cost viable control policy and a [state-dependent] “shadow value” of viability. The policy promotes a large population as a buffer against potential unfavorable stochasticity. The solution method laid out in this paper is also the first to address a number of challenges concerning dynamic programming and joint-chance constrained objectives.

Keywords: endangered species, population viability, chance-constrained dynamic programming

Recreational Leasing of Alaska Commercial Halibut Quota: The Early Years of the GAF Program in Alaska

In Coastal Management (2019). With Kailin Kroetz, Daniel Lew and James Sanchirico

The Pacific Halibut Catch Sharing Plan formalized the halibut allocation process between the Alaska commercial and recreational charter sectors. A new program allowing for inter-sectoral trading let charter operators relax client harvest restrictions (on additional or size-restricted fish, or fishing on closure days). In the first two years of lease market activity, charter participation was fairly low compared to within commercial sector trading, and many of those who participated were identified as self-leasers. The value-per-pound may be higher in the charter sector, as commercial-to-charter transfer prices approached the commercial ex-vessel price.

Keywords: Alaska, Pacific halibut, individual transferable quota, inter-sector quota allocation, Guided Angler Fish, commercial and recreational fishery management
